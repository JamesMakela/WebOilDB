How Information is Used

The information collected by the Oil Library web application is used primarily to aid in the research of petroleum chemical properties, and to be used as input to environmental models. We make every effort to disclose clearly how information is used at the point where it is collected so that our users can determine for themselves whether they wish to provide the information.

Retention of Information

The primary purpose of the Oil Library database is to retain records of oil properties. We may retain these records for archival purposes.

Cookies

"Cookies" are small bits of text that are either used for the duration of a session ("session cookies"), or saved on a user's hard drive in order to identify that user, or information about that user, the next time the user logs on to a website ("persistent cookies"). The Oil Library web application uses cookies that expire after 24 hours and does not use any other persistent tracking technology. Lastly, one or more of our component sites may use session cookies to provide streamlined navigation. These session cookies are deleted from the component's server after your session ends and information from them is not collected or saved. All information entered by the user into this application that is not part of an oil properties record, is only maintained until the Oil Library web application is closed.
